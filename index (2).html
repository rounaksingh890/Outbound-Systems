§ 01INFRASTRUCTURE · DELIVERABILITY

The work that happens before any campaign sends.

Most agencies will sell you a sequence and a SaaS bill. The actual work happens upstream. SPF that resolves cleanly inside the 10-lookup limit. DKIM that signs every message your sequencer sends. DMARC set to monitor first, then quarantine when you've earned it. A custom tracking domain so opens don't poison your root. None of it is hard. Almost no one ships it correctly the first time.

SPF · sender policy framework @ root · TXT
resolves · 6 lookups
; v=spf1 record · root domain @ 3600 IN TXT "v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com include:smartlead.amazonses.com include:_spf.smartlead.ai include:spf.zoho.in ~all"
N · 01
10-lookup ceiling.
SPF fails silently past ten DNS lookups. I flatten with a tool when needed and keep room for a future include.
N · 02
~all, not -all.
Soft-fail during warm-up so a misconfigured include doesn't bin every legit message. Tighten to -all only after DMARC alignment is clean for two weeks.
N · 03
Sub-domain isolation.
Outbound runs on a dedicated send domain (mail.* or hi.*) so cold-email reputation never touches transactional traffic.
DKIM · signing key smartlead._domainkey · TXT
signed · 2048-bit RSA
; smartlead selector · public key smartlead._domainkey 3600 IN TXT "v=DKIM1; k=rsa; p= MIIBIjANBgkqhkiG9w0BAQEFAAOCAQ8AMIIBCgKCAQEA 5kQfZ1H7tXG3rQzN8cVm2YwLpJxK4FrTjNvBhE7sQa 9XmCdRyP6LkW2JhBnVcHsTpYqMfKvAeXuDgFwUiOnQ ... 1024 bits truncated for view ... wIDAQAB"
N · 01
2048-bit, not 1024.
Microsoft started flagging 1024-bit keys as low-confidence in 2024. Smartlead and Instantly both publish 2048 by default. Verify before going live.
N · 02
Selector per sender.
A separate selector for each sending platform (smartlead._domainkey, google._domainkey) means rotating one key never breaks the other.
N · 03
Verify with dig.
After publishing, dig +short TXT smartlead._domainkey.yourdomain.com should return the full key. If it returns nothing, the host has split it across multiple TXT records.
DMARC · reporting policy _dmarc · TXT
aligned · pct=100
; reporting + alignment policy _dmarc 3600 IN TXT "v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; pct=100; rua=mailto:[email protected]; ruf=mailto:[email protected]; aspf=s; adkim=s; fo=1; sp=quarantine"
N · 01
Ramp the percentage.
Week 1: p=none, pct=100. Watch the rua reports. Week 3: p=quarantine, pct=10. Week 5: pct=100. Week 8: p=reject if alignment is clean.
N · 02
Strict alignment.
aspf=s and adkim=s mean the From: domain has to match the SPF return-path and the DKIM d= exactly. Catches forwarders that silently relax alignment.
N · 03
Read the rua reports.
Postmark, EasyDMARC, or a custom parser. Every Tuesday morning. The reports tell you which sender is failing alignment before Gmail does.
Tracking · custom CNAME track · CNAME
SSL · auto-renewed
; custom tracking domain · keeps reputation off the root track 3600 IN CNAME open.smartlead.ai. link 3600 IN CNAME click.smartlead.ai. ; reply tracking on dedicated mailbox reply 3600 IN MX 10 mx.zoho.in. reply 3600 IN TXT "forward=ops@..."
N · 01
Off the root, always.
A tracking domain that uses your bare root means every open ping ties back to your transactional reputation. Sub-domain isolation matters here too.
N · 02
SSL is mandatory.
Gmail flags HTTP tracking pixels as suspicious. The CNAME must support HTTPS. Most platforms auto-provision via Let's Encrypt; verify it before launching.
N · 03
Reply routing.
Replies hit a dedicated mailbox, get parsed for sentiment, then post to Zoho with the conversation thread intact. No "thanks but no thanks" sliding into the founder's inbox at midnight.
Warm-up · 12-week burn smartlead · warmer pool
day 84 · target reached

The 12-week ramp.

A new domain has zero reputation. Send 200 cold emails on day one and the inboxes close. The warmer pool sends from your address to other reputable inboxes, gets the messages opened and replied to, and slowly raises the daily ceiling.

Day 1: 5 sends. Day 14: 20. Day 42: 60. Day 84: 100. After that the inbox can carry a real campaign without provider throttling.

Three rules I follow without exception: dedicated send domain, four to six inboxes per domain max, and one rest day a week even on a warmed inbox.
